1. Vahan 4.0: Mastering Vehicle Services

Vahan 4.0 is the centralized database of all registered vehicles in India. It is designed to act as a unified repository for vehicle information, ensuring that data is accessible to RTOs, police, and banks across state lines.

How to Check RC Status (Registration Certificate)

Checking your RC status is the primary use case for the Vahan portal. This is essential when buying a second-hand car to verify the owner's name, vehicle age, and fuel type.

  1. Step 1: Go to the Parivahan website and select "Know Your Vehicle Details".
  2. Step 2: Create an account using your mobile number (OTP verification is required).
  3. Step 3: Enter the vehicle number (e.g., DL01AB1234) and the Captcha code.
  4. Step 4: Click "Search Vehicle".

The system will display the Owner Name (partially masked for privacy), Registration Date, Model, Class, Fuel Norms (BS4/BS6), and Insurance Validity.

Online Transfer of Ownership

Selling a vehicle? You no longer need to visit the RTO multiple times. The transfer process involves:

  • Form 29 & 30: These are the standard forms for notice of transfer and report of transfer.
  • Aadhaar Authentication: In many states (like Delhi, UP, and Bihar), you can complete the transfer using Aadhaar e-KYC without visiting the RTO, provided your mobile number is linked.
  • Fees: You must pay the transfer fee online via the Vahan payment gateway.

Renewal of Registration (Green Tax)

Private vehicles typically have a registration validity of 15 years. After this, the RC must be renewed every 5 years. Commercial vehicles also require a valid Fitness Certificate. If your vehicle is older than 15 years, you may be liable to pay "Green Tax" to discourage the use of older, more polluting engines.

2. Sarathi Parivahan: The Driving Licence Hub

Sarathi is the software framework that handles Driving Licences (DL), Learner's Licences (LL), and Conductor Licences. The days of standing in long queues are largely over due to the slot booking system.

Step-by-Step: Applying for a Learner's Licence (LL)

Before you get a permanent DL, you must hold a Learner's Licence for at least 30 days. Here is the modern workflow:

  • Method A (Contactless): If you use Aadhaar authentication, you can take the Online LL Test from home. The test monitors you via your webcam. If you pass, the LL is generated instantly.
  • Method B (RTO Visit): If you do not use Aadhaar, you must book a slot, visit the RTO, capture biometrics, and take the test on their computers.

Booking a DL Test Slot

Once you have held your LL for 30 days, you can apply for a Permanent Licence. You must book a slot for the driving skill test:

  1. Visit Sarathi Parivahan > Appointments > Slot Booking > DL Test Slot Booking.
  2. Enter your Application Number and Date of Birth.
  3. Select your vehicle class (MCWG for bikes, LMV for cars).
  4. Choose a date and time (green slots are available).

Pro Tip: Slots fill up very fast (often within minutes of opening at 10 AM or 11 AM depending on the state). Be ready with your details beforehand.

International Driving Permit (IDP)

Indian citizens planning to drive abroad can apply for an IDP via Sarathi. The IDP is valid for one year. You will need a valid Indian DL, Passport, and Visa to apply.

3. E-Challan System & Online Payment

The E-Challan system utilizes CCTV cameras and handheld devices used by traffic police to issue fines digitally. This ensures transparency and real-time updates.

How to Check Pending Challans?

Never ignore a challan, as it can lead to a court summons or increased penalties.

  1. Visit the Parivahan E-Challan website.
  2. You can search by Challan Number, Vehicle Number, or DL Number.
  3. If searching by Vehicle Number, you must enter the last 5 digits of the Chassis or Engine number for security.

Virtual Courts

If you fail to pay a challan within 60 days, it is often sent to a Virtual Court. At this stage, you cannot pay via the standard portal. You must visit the Virtual Courts website, where a judge may lower the fine or issue a summons. You can pay the court-ordered fine online instantly.

4. HSRP (High Security Registration Plates)

The Ministry has made HSRP mandatory for all vehicles. HSRPs are aluminum plates with a chromium-based hologram, a unique laser-branded code, and snap-locks that cannot be removed without destruction.

Why is HSRP Mandatory?

HSRP prevents vehicle theft and counterfeiting. Since the lock mechanism is unique and the plate is linked to your database entry in Vahan, it makes swapping plates on stolen cars extremely difficult.

How to Book HSRP?

Booking HSRP is not done on the main Parivahan website for most states. It is handled by authorized private vendors:

  • BookMyHSRP: For vehicles in Delhi, UP, Himachal, etc.
  • MakeMyHSRP: For other selected regions.
  • SIAM: The Society of Indian Automobile Manufacturers website acts as a directory to find the correct vendor for your car brand.

You must also order a Colour-Coded Fuel Sticker (Blue for Petrol/CNG, Orange for Diesel) along with the plate.

6. The Power of mParivahan App

The mParivahan mobile application is a game-changer for Indian citizens. It allows you to carry digital versions of your documents on your smartphone, which are legally equivalent to the physical hard copies.

Key Features:

  • Virtual RC & DL: You can download your RC and DL into the app. This generates a QR code. If stopped by traffic police, you can show this QR code. The police officer can scan it to verify validity.
  • Share RC: If a family member is borrowing your car, you can use the "Share RC" feature to temporarily allow them to show the document on their phone.
  • Search DL/RC: Verify details of any vehicle parked illegally or involved in an accident just by entering the number plate.

Did you know?

According to the amendment in the Central Motor Vehicles Rules (1989), electronic documents presented via mParivahan or DigiLocker are deemed legally valid. Traffic police cannot demand physical documents if the digital version is valid and verifiable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is mParivahan valid for traffic police?

Yes, according to government notifications, digital documents (RC and DL) shown via the **mParivahan app** or **DigiLocker** are legally valid and accepted by traffic police authorities.

How can I pay my road tax online?

You can pay your road tax by visiting the "Check Post Tax" section on the Parivahan website, selecting your state, and entering your vehicle registration number.
